Overview

The "Sample Subway" is a specialized tool designed to scrape and collect a 30-80mg metal sample from the inside of a nuclear reactor pressure tube. The sample is used to analyze hydrogen levels, a key indicator of structural integrity.

Our solution operates remotely to minimize operator exposure to radiation. It features a self-sealing containment system that weighs the sample in real-time, ensuring precise collection without direct human contact.

Key Features

  • Remote Operation: Reduces radiation exposure for operators.
  • Automated Sampling: Precise scraping mechanism targets 30-80mg samples.
  • Real-time Feedback: Integrated load cells and sensors report status to the operator.
  • Fail-Safe Design: Winch retrieval system ensures the tool can be recovered if power fails.
  • Self-Contained Power: On-board battery system eliminates trailing cables.

Design Process

1. Empathize

Operators in nuclear facilities work under extreme constraints: high radiation levels, limited time windows, and restricted physical access. Understanding the danger and stress of this environment was crucial. The primary user need was safetyâ€”ensuring no direct contact with the radioactive sample while maintaining operational efficiency.

2. Define

We defined the core problem: How to remotely extract a specific mass (30-80mg) from a 4-inch diameter tube, 15 feet deep, without manual intervention? The solution required a self-contained, wireless robot capable of:

  • Traversing the tube horizontally.
  • Scraping a precise sample.
  • Weighing it in real-time.
  • Returning safely, even in power failure.

3. Ideate & Concept

We explored multiple scraping mechanisms, from rotary cutters to linear scrapers. Hand-drawn sketches helped visualize the "Subway" form factorâ€”a cylindrical carriage that could ride inside the tube.

4. Prototype & Test

We moved to CAD and 3D printing to test fit and function. The "Sample Subway" prototype integrated a high-torque DC motor for the scraper and a load cell for real-time weighing. Testing verified that our scraping mechanism could consistently achieve the +/- 9.5mg accuracy required.
